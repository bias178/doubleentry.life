The project

About Double Entry Life

A project about what accounting can teach us about everyday decisions, told through a fictional character and real financial concepts.

Why this exists

Accounting is not just for companies. Every financial decision you make has a double entry. One side shows up in your bank account. The other one shapes your future. Most people only ever read one side.

Double Entry Life exists to show the other side, using the same concepts that accountants use every day, applied to decisions that everyone makes: buying a car, paying rent, saving for the future, choosing a job.

Who is Bill

Bill is a fictional character: 38 years old, 15 years of work experience, a mortgage, a financed car, 25,000 in savings and a vague feeling that his money goes somewhere he cannot quite see.

He is not a cautionary tale. He is not a success story. He is a normal person making normal decisions with incomplete information. Every article gives him one more piece.

The format

Each article is a moment in Bill's life. A decision, a number, a concept. The ledger shows what changes. The insight explains why it matters. You can start anywhere, because every article is designed to stand on its own.

"Bill grows with the story. You can start with yours."
