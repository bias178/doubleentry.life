The Double Entry Life Method

A simple and rigorous way to read how your resources, choices and impacts accumulate into a personal balance over time.

Why use the language of balance sheets

Balance sheets offer clarity. They separate what you have, what you owe and how your decisions affect your long term position. Using this language for personal life helps you see patterns that are usually invisible.

Visible and invisible resources

Money is only one part of your personal balance. Time, energy, skills, relationships, attention and opportunities are also resources that shape your net worth.

Assets and liabilities

Assets are resources that support your goals. Liabilities are commitments that reduce your flexibility or require future effort. Both can be financial or non financial.

Personal income statement

Your personal income statement shows what produces value and what consumes it. It includes income, expenses, habits, routines, interruptions and emotional load.

Personal balance sheet

Your balance sheet is the result of months and years of choices. It includes financial assets, skills, relationships, health, reputation and resilience.

Opportunity cost

Every choice has a cost that is not visible in money. Opportunity cost is the value of the best alternative you give up.

Sustainability over time

A good personal balance is sustainable. It does not rely on constant effort or sacrifice. It grows through consistent, realistic choices.

Limits of the method

This method is a lens, not a formula. It does not replace financial, medical, psychological or professional advice.

Visual model

Assets

Money, time, energy, skills, relationships, attention.

Liabilities

Commitments, debt, obligations, routines that drain energy.

Personal Net Worth

The result of your assets minus your liabilities, visible and invisible.
